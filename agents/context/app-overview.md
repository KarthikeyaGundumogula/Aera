# 🎬 FrameHouse — Product Vision & System Blueprint

---

## 🌌 1. Introduction

FrameHouse is a digital theatre for cinematic expressions—a space where audiences evolve into Artists, and works become expression.

---

## 🧠 Core Belief

Stories don’t end when the credits roll.
They evolve through those who experience them.

FrameHouse exists to capture that evolution.

Its mission is to create a space where fans reinterpret media, build identity through meaning, and are recognized for their creativity—not just their content.

👉 It is a **Living Cinema**

---

## 🎯 Core Philosophy

> **Discovery begins with interaction. Identity is defined by recognition.**

* Interaction introduces users to the system
* Recognition defines who they become

---

## 🎨 2. Expression System

Artists create from existing media called **Works**:

### 🎬 Edits

* video sequences
* cinematic expressions
* mood, pacing, storytelling through cuts

### 🖼️ Posters

* visual redesigns
* alternate perspectives
* symbolic expressions

### 📝 Theories

* fan theories
* narrative expansions
* analytical or poetic breakdowns

---

### Each Work:

* references its **Origins** (source material)
* can receive **Likes, Comments, and Credits**

---

## 🏆 3. Interaction & Recognition System

FrameHouse operates on two layers:

---

### 🔹 Layer 1: Interaction (Onboarding Layer)

Familiar social actions make the platform intuitive:

#### ❤️ Likes

* instant reactions
* low effort
* generic engagement
* help users feel native

#### 💬 Comments

* conversations and reactions
* expression of thoughts and interpretations
* no weight or recognition value

👉 Purpose:

* reduce friction
* encourage participation
* enable discovery

---

### 🔹 Layer 2: Recognition (Core System)

#### 🏅 Credits

* intentional recognition from one artist to another
* require thought before giving
* carry significantly higher weight than likes

Key Rules:

* not available immediately to all users
* unlocked only after gaining **Presence**

👉 This ensures:

* recognition is earned
* signal remains meaningful
* spam is prevented

---

### ⚖️ System Balance

| Layer       | Action   | Nature              | Impact                |
| ----------- | -------- | ------------------- | --------------------- |
| Interaction | Likes    | instant, low effort | discovery / reach     |
| Interaction | Comments | conversational      | engagement            |
| Recognition | Credits  | intentional         | identity / reputation |

👉 Principle:

* Likes drive visibility
* Credits define identity

---

## ☀️ 4. Presence System

Presence is the core measure of an artist’s credibility.

### Defined As:

* the accumulation of **Credits received**

### Not influenced by:

* Likes
* Comments

---

### Properties:

* assigned to profiles, not individual works
* represents long-term artistic impact

---

### Role of Presence:

1. **Unlock Mechanism**

   * users must gain Presence to give Credits

2. **Weight System**

   * the higher the Presence, the more impactful their Credits

👉 Result:
A **self-regulating recognition economy**

---

## 🧾 5. Attribution System — Origins

Every work references its Origins:

* movies
* series
* anime
* music
* original creators

---

### Important Distinction:

* **Origins = Attribution**
* **Credits = Recognition**

They are completely separate systems.

## 🧬 6. Identity System

An Artist’s identity is defined by:

* Credits received
* Who recognized them
* Type of work created

---

👉 Identity is:

* self-shaped
* meaning-driven
* reputation-based

---

The onboarding gate into the Artist community.

* portrait, stage name, and tagline (mission statement)
* social linkage

👉 Result:
Audience members officially "claim their identity" and become Artists.

## 🎨 8. Content Layer

Supported formats:

* Edits (video)
* Posters (image)
* Theories (text)

---

### Card System

All content maps into 4 containers:

* IMAX → cinematic hero
* WIDE → standard edits
* VERTICAL → mobile edits
* SQUARE → theories/posters

---

👉 Principles:

* no distortion
* no padding
* immersive viewing

---

## 🧱 9. Theatre 
### Desktop Experience

### Concept:

A 2D infinite cinematic canvas

---

### Features:

* multidirectional exploration
* spatial navigation
* cluster-based layout

---

### Grid System:

* 12-column structure
* 9-row fixed clusters

---

### Clusters:

* fixed size
* contain multiple cards
* tile seamlessly

---

👉 Result:
An infinite cinematic space with no gaps

---

## 🧠 10. Layout Engine

FrameHouse uses deterministic cluster-based layout.

### Rules:

* max 2 edits per cluster
* at least 1 theory (breathing space)
* posters as medium-weight elements

---

👉 Goal:
Visual rhythm over density

---

## 🌌 11. Interaction Model

* users enter through Likes and Comments
* gradually discover deeper mechanics
* transition into giving and receiving Credits

---

👉 Experience:

* not scrolling content
* entering a space

---

## 🎬 12. Originals System

Each movie/series is a **FrameHouse Original**

---

### Flow:

1. Select a movie (e.g., Dune)
2. Curate top works
3. Build themed theatre
4. Feature selected artists
5. Artists share → growth

---

👉 Outcome:
Organic acquisition loop

---

## 🎯 13. Theme System

Each Original applies:

* background
* color palette
* typography

---

### Important:

* structure remains constant
* only mood changes

---

👉 Result:
consistency with emotional variation
