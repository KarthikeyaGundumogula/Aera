# 🎬 FrameHouse — Product Vision & System Blueprint

---

## 🌌 1. Introduction

FrameHouse is a digital theatre for cinematic expressions. It is a space where fans reinterpret media and build identity through meaning.
It is a **Living Cinema**.

---

## 🎨 2. Expression System

Artists create from existing media called **Works**:

### 🎬 Edits
- video sequences
- cinematic expressions
- mood, pacing, storytelling through cuts

### 🖼️ Posters
- visual redesigns
- alternate perspectives
- symbolic expressions

### 📝 Theories / Scripts
- visual explainers
- narrative expansions
- cinematic storyboard library (up to 10 images)
- analytical or poetic breakdowns

---

### Each Work:
- references its **Origins** (source material)
- can receive **Stars** and **Recommendation Scores**

---

## 🏆 3. Interaction System

FrameHouse operates on meaningful, cinematic interactions rather than generic engagement metrics.

### ⭐ Stars
- The primary interaction metric for Works.
- Replaces standard likes with a more intentional gesture of appreciation.

### 📈 Recommendation Scores (Resonance Hold)
- **Mechanic**: Generated by holding a button (exponential growth). `rate = BASE_RATE * (ACCELERATION ^ secondsHeld)`
- **Peak Crossing UX**: As the score approaches the user's `lastPeakScore`, the UI physically shakes (Framer Motion `useSpring`). Crossing the peak triggers a radial amber flash.
- **Meaning**: A signal of how much an Original moved the recommender, not a quality rating.

---

## 🧾 4. Attribution System — Origins

Every work references its Origins:
- movies, series, anime, music, original creators
**Origins = Attribution**.

---

## 🧬 5. Identity System

An Artist’s identity is defined by the type of work created and their curation.

### 🎭 The Identity Rite
The onboarding gate into the Artist community.
- audiences configure their public persona (portrait, stage name, and tagline)
- social linkage

### 📋 Watchlist Ledger
A dedicated interaction layer for curating Works and Originals.
- Visual confirmations (Toasts) for every addition.
- Accessible via a dedicated cinematic vertical stack view.
- Ledger entries populate the user's Hall curation.

### 🏛️ The Wall (Artist Profile)
A highly curated, persistent "moodboard" or exhibition space on an Artist's profile.
- Artists can post raw text, or **Pin** Works/Originals with a "Director's Note".
- **Distribution**: Wall posts are **NOT** broadcasted globally. They only go to the "Foyer" of users who have explicitly *Favorited* the Artist, creating an intimate, high-trust loop.

---

## 🧑‍🤝‍🧑 6. Community Layer

### 🔹 Sets
- micro-communities centered around themes, movies, or ideas.
- **The Set Wall**: A communal space where members can post. 
- **Cards (Visual Reactions)**: To combat toxicity and maintain a Dark-Luxury aesthetic, **raw text is strictly banned** on the Set Wall. Users communicate via high-fidelity, cinematic visual templates (using GIFs or beautifully typography-driven stills) called **Cards**.

### 🔹 Captains
- curators and moderators who maintain quality and direction

### 🔹 Festivals
- competitions on sets and screens

---

## 🚀 7. The Release Rite (Upload Workflow)

Submitting work is treated as a premium "Post Production" calibration and transmission process. It follows a strict 5-step state machine:
1. **The Identity (`INTEL`)**: Select category (Edit vs Poster) and title.
2. **The Transmission (`TRANSMISSION`)**: Select source (YouTube/Twitter) and paste absolute link. `extractSrcId` and `buildEmbedUrl` handle normalization.
3. **The Canvas (`PROJECT`)**: Assign to an Original (populates `originalIds`).
4. **The Geometry (`GEOMETRY`)**: Select aspect ratio (IMAX, Academy, Vertical, Square). Renders 1:1 Live Preview.
5. **The Seal (`SEAL`)**: Final verification and backend sync.

---

## 🎨 8. Content Layer & Container Formats

Supported formats map into 4 containers:
- **IMAX** → cinematic hero (16:9 Video only)
- **WIDE** → standard edits (4:3)
- **VERTICAL** → mobile edits (9:16)
- **SQUARE** → theories/posters (1:1)

👉 Principles: no distortion, no padding, immersive viewing.

---

## 🧱 9. Theatre & Frontend Architecture

### Concept: A 2D infinite cinematic canvas

### Grid System (Desktop):
- 12-column structure with 9-row fixed clusters.
- Multidirectional exploration.

### Layout Engine:
- FrameHouse uses deterministic cluster-based layout.
- max 2 edits per cluster, at least 1 theory (breathing space).
- Goal: Visual rhythm over density.

### Typography & Title Rules (The 95% Rule):
- Title containers are restricted to a max 95% width. Single words shrink-to-fit; multi-words wrap upward.

### Modal & UX Mechanics:
- **Decentralized Modals**: `EditWork`, `PosterWork`, and `ScriptWork` components manage their own internal modal states. Clicking a card anywhere (Theatre, Upload preview) triggers the modal perfectly.
- **3D Flip Mechanic**: Works feature a physical card flip to reveal metadata (Artist, Original, Format) on the back.
- **Universal Scroll Resilience (iOS Fix)**: When modals open, the `body` is instantly set to `position: fixed` with its top offset to `-window.scrollY` to prevent momentum scrolling bleed-through on mobile Safari.

## 🎬 10. Originals System

Each movie/series is a **FrameHouse Original**
Flow: Select a movie -> Curate top works -> Build themed theatre -> Feature artists.

---

## 🎯 11. Theme System

Each Original applies a specific background, color palette, and typography.
*Structure remains constant, only mood changes.*

---

## 🧩 12. Design Philosophy

### Core Principles:
- Consistency over novelty
- Expression over content
- Identity over engagement
- Space over density

### Visual Philosophy:
- structured chaos, cinematic rhythm, intentional silence

---

## 🗣️ 13. Discussions (Sequences)

FrameHouse explicitly separates viewing art from debating it.
- **The Screen (Theatre):** Immersive, dark, purely for **Works**. Interaction is limited to viewing and reacting.
- **The Sets (Discussions):** Deep, community-owned discussion threads (Sequences) live natively inside Sets, pushing heavy discussions into dedicated spaces rather than cluttering the global feed.

---

## 🎬 Final Statement

FrameHouse is not a social feed. It is a cinematic space where fans become Artists, and Artists build meaning.
